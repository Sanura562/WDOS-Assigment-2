Indigenous Animals in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka is home to a diverse array of indigenous animals, showcasing a rich biodiversity that spans various ecosystems. Here's a brief overview of some notable indigenous animals found in Sri Lanka:

Rusty-spotted Cat

The rusty-spotted cat is one of the smallest wild cat species in the world and is endemic to India and Sri Lanka. It is characterized by its small size, rusty-colored coat, and spotted markings. These elusive cats are found in various habitats, including grasslands and scrub jungles.

Sloth Bear

The sloth bear is a medium-sized bear species found in Sri Lanka. These bears have a distinctive appearance, with a shaggy coat and a white "V" or "Y" mark on their chest. They inhabit dry forests and are known for their unique feeding habits

Purple-faced Langur

The purple-faced langur, also known as the purple-faced leaf monkey, is an endemic primate species found in the rainforests of Sri Lanka. Their distinctive feature is the purple skin on their face, which becomes more prominent as they age.
